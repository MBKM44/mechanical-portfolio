# Finalized Slide Content: Rice 1968 J-Integral

## Slide 1 - Rice 1968: The J-Integral

**Slide text**

- A path-independent integral for cracks and notches
- J. R. Rice, 1968
- One contour measure for crack-tip severity

**Visual description**

Cracked plate with remote tension arrows. Two differently sized contours surround the crack tip, both labeled `J`.

## Slide 2 - Why This Paper Matters

**Slide text**

- Failure starts near the crack tip
- Loads and geometry are known far away
- Plasticity makes the local field hard to solve

**Visual description**

Left side: specimen under tension. Right side: zoom bubble showing the crack tip and a small high-strain zone.

## Slide 3 - The Problem Before the Paper

**Slide text**

- Linear elastic fracture mechanics had `K` and energy release rate
- Elastic-plastic metals develop concentrated strain near the tip
- Full nonlinear boundary-value solutions are difficult

**Visual description**

Crack tip with an irregular plastic zone and small stress/strain arrows. The local field is intentionally shown as complex.

## Slide 4 - Rice's Core Move

**Slide text**

- Draw any valid contour around the tip
- Integrate stored energy and traction work
- The value is the same: `J_1 = J_2 = J_3`

**Visual description**

Three nested contours around one crack tip, each tagged with the same J.

## Slide 5 - Intuitive Example

**Slide text**

- Far path: easier loading information
- Near path: crack-tip deformation severity
- Path independence connects them

**Visual description**

Side-by-side panels: far contour around the crack in a larger body and near contour around the tip, joined by an equals sign.

## Slide 6 - The Essential Equation

**Slide text**

`J = integral_Gamma (W dy - T . du/dx ds)`

- `W`: strain energy density
- `T`: traction on the contour
- `u`: displacement
- `x`: crack-extension direction

**Visual description**

Equation centered, with callout lines from each symbol to a simple contour schematic.

## Slide 7 - What J Connects

**Slide text**

- Energy rate: `J = -dP/da`
- Small-scale yielding: `J = (1 - nu^2)K_I^2/E`
- Near-tip severity: averaged local deformation measure

**Visual description**

Three-node network: Energy release, stress intensity K, crack-tip deformation. J sits in the center.

## Slide 8 - What Changed, and What It Does Not Solve

**Slide text**

- Enabled elastic-plastic fracture characterization
- Led into HRR crack-tip fields
- Assumptions: 2D, no body forces, traction-free crack faces
- Limitation: deformation plasticity, not full loading-history plasticity

**Visual description**

Compact timeline: Griffith/Irwin -> Rice J -> HRR fields -> modern elastic-plastic fracture testing.

## Slide 9 - Final Takeaway

**Slide text**

- A hard local crack problem becomes a contour-energy measure
- Evaluate J where it is convenient
- Interpret J where failure begins

**Visual description**

Large crack-tip contour graphic with final statement: "One contour integral. One crack-driving measure."
